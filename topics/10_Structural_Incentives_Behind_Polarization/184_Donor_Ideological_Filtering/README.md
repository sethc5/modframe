# Donor Ideological Filtering

Purpose: Maps how ideologically organized donor networks make conformity to factional priorities a component of candidates' fundraising efficiency, creating structural financial incentives for ideological alignment across the candidate recruitment-to-legislative career pipeline.

Status: draft

Tags: campaign finance, donor networks, bundlers, ideological filtering, Koch network, EMILY's List, Citizens United, polarization

Outline: see `outline.md`

## Key actors

- Candidates for federal office (network access-seekers)
- Major donors and donor councils (network gatekeepers)
- Bundlers (aggregation intermediaries)
- Ideological PACs and super PACs (Club for Growth, EMILY's List, Chamber of Commerce)
- Party campaign committees (approved vendor and endorsed candidate pipelines)
- FEC (disclosure regulator)

## Statutes and authorities

- Federal Election Campaign Act, 52 U.S.C. § 30101 et seq.
- *Citizens United v. Federal Election Commission*, 558 U.S. 310 (2010)
- *SpeechNow.org v. Federal Election Commission*, 599 F.3d 686 (D.C. Cir. 2010)

## Case studies

- Koch network donor councils and candidate vetting process (Americans for Prosperity)
- EMILY's List endorsement effect on candidate fundraising access
- Dark money 501(c)(4) contribution networks and disclosure gaps

## Data sources
