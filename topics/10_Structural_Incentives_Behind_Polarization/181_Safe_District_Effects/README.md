# Safe District Effects

Purpose: Maps how non-competitive House districts convert primary electorates into the effective electoral constraint on incumbents, incentivizing ideological positioning at the party extreme rather than the legislative center.

Status: draft

Tags: redistricting, primaries, safe seats, polarization, DW-NOMINATE, competitiveness, electoral structure

Outline: see `outline.md`

## Key actors

- Incumbents in safe seats (primary-constrained legislators)
- Primary challengers (the credible threat mechanism)
- State legislatures and redistricting commissions (draw the map)
- Outside spending groups (Club for Growth, Justice Democrats)
- NRCC, DCCC (party protection decisions)
- Cook Political Report, Sabato's Crystal Ball (competitiveness trackers)

## Statutes and authorities

- Article I, Section 4 (congressional elections authority)
- Voting Rights Act (constraints on redistricting)
- State redistricting statutes and commission enabling laws

## Case studies

- 2011 redistricting cycle and subsequent DW-NOMINATE score shifts
- Arizona and California independent redistricting commission outcomes
- Eric Cantor primary loss (2014) as safe-seat upset demonstration event
