# Political Consultant Economy

Purpose: Maps how the fee structures and economic incentives of the political consulting industry create a professional class whose financial interests align with perpetuating high-intensity, high-spending, high-conflict campaigns rather than efficient democratic competition.

Status: draft

Tags: political consultants, campaign spending, media buyers, fee structures, fundraising vendors, FEC, campaign economy

Outline: see `outline.md`

## Key actors

- Media consultants and ad buyers (commission-based fee structures)
- Digital fundraising vendors (performance-fee email and digital strategy)
- Polling firms (per-project revenue)
- Opposition research firms (adversarial contest dependency)
- Voter data vendors (i360, NGP VAN, TargetSmart)
- Party campaign committees (approved vendor lists)
- FEC (disbursement disclosure regulator)

## Statutes and authorities

- Federal Election Campaign Act, 52 U.S.C. § 30101 et seq. (disbursement disclosure)
- FEC regulations on campaign finance reporting

## Case studies

- Senate campaign media consultant commission structures (FEC disbursement filings)
- DCCC and NRCC approved vendor list restrictions on campaign hiring
- ActBlue/WinRed vendor fee structures disclosed in FEC filings

## Data sources

- FEC disbursement database by vendor (https://www.fec.gov/data/)
- Open Secrets vendor spending analysis (https://www.opensecrets.org)
