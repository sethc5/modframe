# Media Fragmentation

Purpose: Maps how the collapse of shared national news audiences and the rise of partisan media ecosystems creates divergent factual environments for elected officials and citizens, undermining the shared informational basis required for bipartisan legislative agreement.

Status: draft

Tags: media, polarization, cable news, social media, information environment, local news, FCC

Outline: see `outline.md`

## Key actors

- Cable news networks (Fox News, MSNBC, CNN)
- Social media platforms (algorithmic content distribution)
- Members of Congress and communications staff (outlet selection)
- Local news organizations (declining coverage capacity)
- FCC (broadcast regulation)
- Pew Research Center, Reuters Institute (audience measurement)

## Statutes and authorities

- Communications Act of 1934 (FCC regulatory authority)
- Telecommunications Act of 1996 (broadcast ownership deregulation)
- First Amendment (forecloses content-based government intervention)

## Case studies

- Pew Research "Political Polarization and Media Habits" longitudinal surveys
- Local news desert expansion (2004–present, Medill Local News Initiative tracking)
- Divergent January 6 coverage across partisan cable outlets
