# Primary Threat Leverage

Purpose: Maps how the credible threat of a funded primary challenge disciplines incumbent congressional voting behavior without a primary needing to occur, functioning as an invisible enforcement mechanism for ideological orthodoxy.

Status: draft

Tags: primaries, primary threats, outside spending, ideological discipline, Club for Growth, Justice Democrats, polarization

Outline: see `outline.md`

## Key actors

- Incumbent members of Congress (disciplined actors)
- Outside spending groups (Club for Growth, Heritage Action, Justice Democrats, EMILY's List)
- Potential primary challengers (credibility mechanism)
- Party leadership (withholding or providing protection)
- NRCC, DCCC (primary non-interference norms and violations)

## Statutes and authorities

- Federal Election Campaign Act, 52 U.S.C. § 30101 et seq.
- *Citizens United v. FEC*, 558 U.S. 310 (2010) (outside spending authority)

## Case studies

- Eric Cantor (R-VA) primary loss to Dave Brat, June 2014
- Joe Crowley (D-NY) primary loss to Alexandria Ocasio-Cortez, June 2018
- Club for Growth primary threats against Republicans who voted for bipartisan infrastructure packages
