# Fundraising Incentives for Conflict

Purpose: Maps how the small-dollar fundraising infrastructure of congressional campaigns rewards conflict-based messaging over compromise, creating structural financial incentives against legislative resolution of recurring partisan crises.

Status: draft

Tags: campaign finance, fundraising, polarization, small-dollar donors, ActBlue, WinRed, consultant economy

Outline: see `outline.md`

## Key actors

- Members of Congress (primary fundraisers, calibrate messaging to donor response)
- ActBlue / WinRed (platform design shapes fundraising norms)
- Campaign consultants and email vendors (performance-fee incentives)
- Small-dollar donor base (responds to conflict framing)
- Congressional campaign committees (DCCC, NRCC, DSCC, NRSC)
- FEC (disclosure regulator)

## Statutes and authorities

- Federal Election Campaign Act, 52 U.S.C. § 30101 et seq.
- FEC regulations on contribution limits and reporting

## Case studies

- Government shutdown fundraising spikes (2018–2019, 2023)
- Debt ceiling standoff fundraising patterns (2023)
- ActBlue and WinRed processing volume growth relative to conflict event frequency

## Data sources

- Open Secrets campaign finance analysis (https://www.opensecrets.org)
