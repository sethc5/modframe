# Sovereign Wealth Fund Investments

Purpose: Explains how CFIUS mandatory review thresholds and passive-investment exemptions leave open pathways for foreign-government sovereign wealth fund capital to accumulate positions in U.S. technology, infrastructure, and real estate without systematic disclosure or review, and how FIRRMA (2018) expanded but did not close this gap.

Status: draft

Tags: sovereign wealth funds, CFIUS, FIRRMA, foreign investment, national security, Treasury, disclosure

Key actors:
- Committee on Foreign Investment in the United States (CFIUS)
- Treasury Department (CFIUS secretariat)
- Department of Defense and Intelligence Community (CFIUS participants)
- Norway Government Pension Fund Global, Abu Dhabi Investment Authority, China Investment Corporation, Saudi Arabia PIF
- SEC (13F disclosure)
- Congressional oversight (Senate Banking, House Financial Services)

Statutes and authorities:
- Foreign Investment Risk Review Modernization Act of 2018 (FIRRMA), Pub. L. 115-232, §§ 1701-1728
- CFIUS regulations: 31 C.F.R. Parts 800, 801 (effective February 2020)
- Defense Production Act of 1950, as amended, 50 U.S.C. § 4565
- Securities Exchange Act § 13(f) (institutional investor 13F disclosure)

Case studies:
- Grand Forks, ND Chinese corn mill acquisition (2022) — CFIUS real estate/military proximity gap
- SoftBank Vision Fund (Saudi Arabia PIF-backed) U.S. technology investments
- CFIUS mitigation agreement with Broadcom/Qualcomm (2018)
