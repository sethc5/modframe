# Revolving Door Long Term Influence Chains

Purpose: Maps how multi-decade revolving door careers create compound influence portfolios, where each rotation between government, industry, think tanks, and lobbying increases the individual's access value and institutional knowledge.

Status: draft

Tags: revolving door, influence, lobbying, think tanks, compound access, career trajectories

Outline: see `outline.md`

## Key actors

- Career revolvers (multi-rotation government-industry professionals)
- Lobbying firms recruiting former officials
- Think tanks providing institutional affiliation between rotations
- Government agencies hiring from the private sector
- Regulated industries hiring former regulators

## Statutes and authorities

- 18 U.S.C. § 207 (post-employment restrictions on former government officials)
- Ethics in Government Act of 1978 (financial disclosure, conflict of interest)
- Executive Order 13770 (Trump 2017 ethics pledge) / Executive Order 13490 (Obama 2009 ethics pledge)
- Honest Leadership and Open Government Act of 2007 (cooling-off periods)

## Case studies

- Senior officials with 3+ government-to-private-sector rotations across 20+ year careers
- Defense sector revolving door chains (DOD to defense contractor to think tank to DOD)
- Financial regulation revolving door (SEC/Treasury to Wall Street to policy advisory to government)
