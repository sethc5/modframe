# Donor Advised Fund Political Pathways

Purpose: This module maps how donor-advised funds create a tax-subsidized anonymity layer for political spending, allowing donors to claim immediate deductions while directing grants to politically active organizations without public disclosure of the donor's identity.

Status: draft

Tags: mechanics, institutions, accountability, donor-advised-funds, dark-money, philanthropy, tax-exempt, 501c4

## Key actors
- Individual and corporate DAF donors
- DAF sponsoring organizations (Fidelity Charitable, Schwab, Vanguard, community foundations)
- 501(c)(4) social welfare organizations (politically active recipients)
- IRS (tax-exempt regulation, no DAF donor disclosure)
- Congressional tax-writing committees (reform authority)
- State attorneys general (charitable organization oversight)

## Primary statutes and authorities
- Internal Revenue Code § 4966–4967 (DAF rules)
- IRC § 170 (charitable contribution deductions)
- IRC § 501(c)(4) (social welfare organization exemption)

## Case study
- **DAF grants to politically active 501(c)(4)s**: Analysis of Form 990 data showing grant flows from major DAF sponsoring organizations to 501(c)(4) organizations engaged in election-cycle political advertising, with the original donor identity hidden by the DAF intermediary.

## Data sources
- National Philanthropic Trust annual DAF reports
- IRS Form 990 filings for major DAF sponsoring organizations
- OpenSecrets dark money analysis
- Congressional Research Service DAF reports
