# Political Consultant Compensation Incentives

Purpose: This module maps the compensation structures of political consulting firms — commissions on ad spend, percentage-of-raise fundraising fees, retainers, and subcontracting layers — analyzing how these structures create incentives misaligned with candidate and donor interests.

Status: draft

Tags: mechanics, institutions, accountability, consulting, campaigns, media-buying, fundraising, compensation

## Key actors
- Media buying firms (commission-based ad placement)
- Fundraising consultants (percentage-of-raise compensation)
- Campaign strategists / general consultants
- Pollsters
- Candidates and campaign committees
- Donors
- FEC (expenditure disclosure, not fee structure disclosure)

## Case study
- **Media buying commissions in presidential campaigns**: Analysis of FEC disbursement data showing the percentage of total campaign spending consumed by media buying commissions versus the amount reaching voters through advertising.

## Data sources
- FEC campaign finance disbursement data
- OpenSecrets political consulting industry analysis
- Campaign finance reports (FEC Form 3, Form 3X)
- Academic studies on political consulting economics
