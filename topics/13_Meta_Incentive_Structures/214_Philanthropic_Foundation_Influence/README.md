# Philanthropic Foundation Influence

Purpose: This module maps how large philanthropic foundations shape public policy through grant-making that funds research, advocacy, model legislation, and career pathways, exercising influence comparable to political parties without democratic accountability.

Status: draft

Tags: mechanics, institutions, accountability, philanthropy, foundations, grant-making, tax-exempt, policy-influence

## Key actors
- Foundation boards and leadership (grant-making priorities)
- Foundation program officers (grantee selection and management)
- Grantee organizations (think tanks, advocacy groups, universities)
- IRS (payout requirements, political activity restrictions)
- Congress (tax rules governing foundations)
- State attorneys general (charitable purpose oversight)

## Primary statutes and authorities
- IRC §§ 4940–4948 (private foundation excise tax and payout rules)
- IRC § 501(c)(3) (tax-exempt status requirements)
- IRC § 4945 (restrictions on foundation lobbying and political activity)

## Case study
- **Koch network policy infrastructure**: The Koch-affiliated foundation network's strategic investment in academic programs, think tanks, and advocacy organizations illustrates how foundation grant-making can build a multi-decade policy influence infrastructure across research, education, and advocacy channels.

## Data sources
- Foundation Center / Candid grants database
- IRS Form 990-PF filings for major private foundations
- Academic research on philanthropy and policy influence
- GAO reports on tax-exempt organization oversight
