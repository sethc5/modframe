# Academic Policy Cross Pollination Pathways

Purpose: Maps how universities and academic researchers serve as credibility nodes in the policy influence ecosystem, producing research shaped by funding sources and career incentives that are systematically under-disclosed to policymakers.

Status: draft

Tags: academia, policy research, funding, think tanks, expert testimony, credibility, disclosure

Outline: see `outline.md`

## Key actors

- University-affiliated policy centers and institutes
- Individual academic researchers and expert witnesses
- Federal research funding agencies (NSF, NIH, DOD, DOE)
- Corporate sponsors of university research
- Foundations funding policy research
- Congressional committees (expert testimony consumers)

## Statutes and authorities

- Federal grant disclosure requirements (OMB Uniform Guidance, 2 CFR Part 200)
- Congressional testimony disclosure rules (committee-specific)
- NIH financial conflict of interest regulations (42 CFR Part 50, Subpart F)
- Bayh-Dole Act (university patent rights from federally funded research)

## Case studies

- Corporate-funded university policy centers in energy, finance, and healthcare regulation
- Academic expert testimony before congressional committees with undisclosed industry funding
- Elite policy school graduates entering government service and implementing frameworks learned in their training

## Data sources

- National Science Foundation, federal R&D funding data (https://www.nsf.gov)
- ProPublica, academic conflict-of-interest investigations (https://www.propublica.org)
- Foundation Center / Candid, grants to universities (https://candid.org)
- Congressional testimony records and witness disclosure statements
