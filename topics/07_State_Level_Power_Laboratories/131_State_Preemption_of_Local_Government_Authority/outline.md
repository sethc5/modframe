# State Preemption of Local Government Authority

**Summary:** State preemption—the use of state legislation to override or prohibit local government action on specific policy areas—has accelerated dramatically since 2010, with state legislatures blocking cities and counties from enacting minimum-wage increases, gun regulations, anti-discrimination protections, plastic-bag bans, and sanctuary-city policies. Preemption exploits Dillon's Rule (the legal doctrine that local governments possess only those powers expressly granted by the state), turning the state-local relationship into a tool for imposing uniform policy where local majorities prefer different approaches. The strategic deployment of preemption often correlates with partisan misalignment between state legislatures and urban centers, functioning as a mechanism for rural-dominated legislatures to constrain progressive urban policy experimentation. [Observed]

**Mechanism in one sentence:** State legislatures use preemption statutes to override local government authority, imposing statewide policy uniformity that constrains municipal policy experimentation. [Observed]

### Actors and roles

- State legislatures enact preemption statutes that prohibit local governments from regulating in specified policy domains, often with punitive enforcement provisions. [Observed]
- Governors sign or veto preemption bills, with partisan alignment between governor and legislature determining whether preemption advances. [Observed]
- Municipal governments challenge preemption through litigation, legislative lobbying, and creative regulatory workarounds that test the boundaries of state restrictions. [Observed]
- Industry groups and model-legislation networks (ALEC) advocate for preemption as a strategy to prevent local regulatory patchworks that increase compliance costs. [Observed]
- Local advocacy organizations mobilize against preemption to preserve municipal authority over wages, environmental standards, and civil-rights protections. [Observed]

### Process map (bulleted)

- Industry groups or ideological organizations identify local policies they oppose and develop preemption strategies through model legislation and lobbying campaigns. [Observed]
- State legislators introduce preemption bills that may prohibit specific local actions, void existing ordinances, or impose penalties on local officials who enforce preempted measures. [Observed]
- Legislative committees hear testimony from industry advocates, municipal officials, and affected communities, though preemption bills often advance along party-line votes. [Observed]
- Enacted preemption statutes take effect, voiding existing local ordinances and prohibiting future local action in the covered policy areas. [Observed]
- Municipalities respond through litigation challenging preemption constitutionality, creative regulatory alternatives, or legislative advocacy for home-rule protections. [Observed]

### Where power concentrates

- **Gatekeepers:** State legislative leadership determines which preemption bills receive hearings and floor votes, controlling the scope of local authority reduction. [Observed]
- **Bottlenecks:** Gerrymandered state legislatures amplify rural political power, enabling preemption of urban policies by majorities that do not represent affected populations. [Observed]
- **Veto points:** Punitive preemption provisions that threaten personal liability for local officials create chilling effects that deter local policy experimentation. [Observed]

### Common failure modes

- Preemption creates policy uniformity that prevents local adaptation to diverse economic conditions, community preferences, and demographic circumstances. [Observed]
- Punitive preemption provisions (defunding, personal liability) escalate state-local conflict beyond policy disagreement into institutional coercion. [Observed]
- Preemption of local minimum-wage, paid-leave, or anti-discrimination ordinances disproportionately affects low-income and minority populations concentrated in urban areas. [Observed]
- Blanket preemption may prevent local innovation that could inform more effective state or federal policy through experimentation. [Inferred]
