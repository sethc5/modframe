# Data Privacy Regulatory Structures

Purpose: Explains how the absence of a comprehensive federal data privacy statute leaves regulation to a sectoral patchwork (HIPAA, FCRA, COPPA) with significant gaps for data brokers, commercial platforms, and inferred-attribute data, and why Congress has failed to enact comprehensive legislation despite repeated attempts.

Status: draft

Tags: data privacy, HIPAA, FCRA, data brokers, FTC, California CPRA, GDPR, technology, regulation

Key actors:
- Federal Trade Commission (FTC) — Section 5 unfairness authority
- Department of Health and Human Services (HIPAA enforcement)
- Consumer Financial Protection Bureau (CFPB) — FCRA rulemaking
- Data broker industry (Acxiom, LexisNexis, CoreLogic)
- California Privacy Protection Agency (CPPA)
- European Union (GDPR extraterritorial enforcement)

Statutes and authorities:
- Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act of 1996, Pub. L. 104-191 (HIPAA)
- Fair Credit Reporting Act, 15 U.S.C. § 1681 et seq. (FCRA)
- Children's Online Privacy Protection Act, 15 U.S.C. § 6501 et seq. (COPPA)
- California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) and California Privacy Rights Act (CPRA)
- American Data Privacy and Protection Act (ADPPA) — proposed, not enacted
- General Data Protection Regulation (EU) 2016/679 (extraterritorial effect)

Case studies:
- ADPPA passage through House Energy and Commerce Committee (2022) and Senate failure
- FTC data broker rulemaking under Section 5 unfairness authority
- Roe v. Wade data privacy implications — period-tracking app data and law enforcement requests

Data sources:

- CPPA enforcement actions under California CPRA
- IAPP (International Association of Privacy Professionals) U.S. state law tracker
- GAO reports on data broker regulation
