# Algorithmic Governance Impact on Public Policy

Purpose: Explains how federal agencies deploy algorithmic decision-making systems in consequential public programs without the procedural protections that apply to conventional rulemaking, and what this means for accountability, transparency, and due process.

Status: draft

Tags: algorithms, APA, due process, automation, administrative law, accountability, technology

Key actors:
- Federal agencies deploying algorithmic systems (SSA, DHS, HUD, IRS)
- Office of Information and Regulatory Affairs (OIRA), OMB
- Government Accountability Office (GAO)
- Office of Science and Technology Policy (OSTP)
- Federal courts (automated benefits denial standing questions)

Statutes and authorities:
- Administrative Procedure Act, 5 U.S.C. §§ 551–559
- OMB Memorandum M-24-10 (AI governance in federal agency use)
- AI in Government Act of 2020, Pub. L. 116-260, div. U, § 1001
- Executive Order 14110 (rescinded January 2025)
- OSTP AI Bill of Rights (2022, non-binding)

Case studies:
- SSA automated Continuing Disability Review denials
- ICE Integrated Decision Support (IDS) risk score algorithm
- GAO AI accountability gap audits (GAO-21-519SP and subsequent)

Data sources:
- GAO reports on algorithmic federal systems
- OMB AI use case inventories
- ACLU and Upturn research on automated decision systems
