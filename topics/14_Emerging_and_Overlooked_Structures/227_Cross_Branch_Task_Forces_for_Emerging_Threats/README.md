# Cross-Branch Task Forces for Emerging Threats

Purpose: Explains how executive branch interagency task forces for emerging threats (AI, pandemic, cybersecurity, climate) operate outside APA procedural protections and are structurally reversible by the next administration, creating a governance durability gap.

Status: draft

Tags: interagency coordination, NSC, APA, executive orders, AI, pandemic preparedness, durability, accountability

Key actors:
- National Security Council (NSC) and interagency process
- Office of Science and Technology Policy (OSTP)
- National Economic Council (NEC) and Domestic Policy Council (DPC)
- Office of Management and Budget (OIRA)
- Government Accountability Office (GAO)
- Congressional oversight committees

Statutes and authorities:
- National Security Act of 1947, as amended, 50 U.S.C. § 3021 et seq. (NSC statutory basis)
- Administrative Procedure Act, 5 U.S.C. §§ 551–559
- Executive Order 14110, Safe, Secure, and Trustworthy Development and Use of AI (October 2023; rescinded January 2025)
- OMB Memorandum M-24-10 (agency AI governance)

Case studies:
- EO 14110 AI safety framework creation and January 2025 rescission
- NSC pandemic preparedness directorate (abolished 2018; not reconstituted before COVID-19)
- GAO documented interagency coordination failures across multiple emerging-threat domains

Data sources:
- Executive order archive (Federal Register)
- GAO reports on interagency coordination effectiveness
- Congressional Research Service analysis of interagency coordination mechanisms
- OMB annual report on regulatory review
