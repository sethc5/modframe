# Defense Production Act Activation Rules

Purpose: Maps how the Defense Production Act gives the President standing peacetime authority to direct private industry production, creating wartime-level industrial intervention powers constrained only by political will and institutional capacity.

Status: draft

Tags: DPA, industrial mobilization, production, DPAS, CFIUS, supply chain, presidential authority

Outline: see `outline.md`

## Key actors

- The President (DPA authority delegation)
- Department of Defense (DPAS, defense priorities)
- HHS / ASPR (health emergency production)
- FEMA (allocation logistics)
- Private industry (rated order compliance)
- CFIUS (foreign acquisition review)

## Statutes and authorities

- Defense Production Act of 1950, as amended (50 U.S.C. §§ 4501–4568)
- Title I (priorities and allocations), Title III (production expansion), Title VII (voluntary agreements, CFIUS)
- 50 U.S.C. § 4513 (penalties for non-compliance)

## Case studies

- COVID-19 DPA activation for ventilators, PPE, and vaccines
- CFIUS blocking of foreign acquisitions (e.g., Broadcom/Qualcomm)
- DPA Title III mineral production expansion
