# Emergency Funding Appropriation Pathways

Purpose: Maps how emergency funding pathways bypass normal appropriations oversight to enable rapid crisis response, creating opportunities for unaccountable spending and non-emergency riders.

Status: draft

Tags: emergency funding, appropriations, supplemental, DRF, FEMA, oversight, accountability, procurement

Outline: see `outline.md`

## Key actors

- Congress / Appropriations Committees (supplemental appropriations)
- OMB (emergency designation, supplemental requests)
- FEMA (Disaster Relief Fund management)
- Agency CFOs (spending execution)
- Inspectors General / GAO (post-expenditure audit)
- PRAC (pandemic spending coordination)

## Statutes and authorities

- Budget Control Act of 2011 (emergency designation exemptions)
- Stafford Act (DRF authority)
- CARES Act, American Rescue Plan Act (COVID-19 emergency appropriations)
- Transfer and reprogramming authorities (10 U.S.C. § 2214, various agency-specific)

## Case studies

- COVID-19 supplemental appropriations (CARES Act, $2.2T) and oversight findings
- Hurricane supplemental appropriations with non-emergency riders
- DRF immediate needs funding activations during concurrent disasters
