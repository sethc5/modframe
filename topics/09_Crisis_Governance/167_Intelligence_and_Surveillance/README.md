# Intelligence and Surveillance

Purpose: Maps how intelligence surveillance authorities operate through classified legal interpretations and a secret court system, creating oversight mechanisms that cannot be independently evaluated by the public or most of Congress.

Status: draft

Tags: surveillance, FISA, Section 702, FISC, NSA, intelligence, civil liberties, classification

Outline: see `outline.md`

## Key actors

- National Security Agency (signals intelligence collection)
- Foreign Intelligence Surveillance Court (judicial review)
- Director of National Intelligence (program certification)
- Attorney General (application approval)
- Congressional intelligence committees (SSCI, HPSCI)
- Privacy and Civil Liberties Oversight Board (PCLOB)

## Statutes and authorities

- Foreign Intelligence Surveillance Act (50 U.S.C. §§ 1801–1885c)
- FISA Amendments Act of 2008, Section 702 (50 U.S.C. § 1881a)
- Executive Order 12333 (U.S. Intelligence Activities)
- USA FREEDOM Act of 2015 (FISC amicus provision)

## Case studies

- Snowden disclosures and Section 215 bulk metadata program
- Section 702 reauthorization debates (2017-2018, 2023-2024)
- FISC compliance violation reports and corrective actions

## Data sources

- DNI, Annual Statistical Transparency Report (https://www.dni.gov)
- PCLOB, Section 215 and Section 702 reports (https://www.pclob.gov)
- FISC, declassified opinions and orders (https://www.fisc.uscourts.gov)
