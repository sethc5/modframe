# Glossary

A shared vocabulary so modules stay consistent.

- **Gatekeeper**: actor or mechanism that controls access to a process or outcome.
- **Bottleneck**: step where throughput is constrained (time, budget, attention, staffing).
- **Veto point**: place where an actor can block progress.
- **Principalâ€“agent problem**: agents act in their own interest when principals can't monitor effectively.
- **Regulatory capture**: regulated interests exert outsized influence over regulators.
- **Transparency gap**: missing or delayed information that prevents accountability.
- **Market maker**: firm that continuously quotes bid and ask prices, providing liquidity in exchange for the spread.
- **Clearing house**: central counterparty that interposes itself between buyer and seller to guarantee trade settlement and manage counterparty risk.
- **Prime broker**: division of a large bank that provides leveraged trading, securities lending, and custody services to hedge funds and institutional investors.
- **Fiduciary duty**: legal obligation to act in the best interest of another party (e.g., a client, beneficiary, or shareholder).
- **Systemic risk**: risk that the failure of one institution or market participant triggers cascading failures across the financial system.
- **Moral hazard**: incentive for an actor to take excessive risk when the costs of failure are borne by others (e.g., taxpayers, depositors, counterparties).
- **Too big to fail**: condition where an institution's size and interconnectedness make its disorderly failure unacceptable to regulators, creating implicit government backstop incentives.
- **Dark pool**: private trading venue that allows institutional investors to execute large orders without pre-trade price transparency on public exchanges.
- **Payment for order flow**: practice where a broker routes customer orders to a market maker in exchange for compensation, creating potential conflicts between execution quality and broker revenue.
