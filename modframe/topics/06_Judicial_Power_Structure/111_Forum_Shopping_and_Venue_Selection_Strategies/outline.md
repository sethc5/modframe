# Forum Shopping and Venue Selection Strategies

**Summary:** Federal venue and jurisdiction rules allow litigants to select among multiple courts for filing lawsuits, creating strategic opportunities to choose judges, circuits, and procedural environments most favorable to desired outcomes. Single-judge divisions in certain federal districts—most notably the Northern District of Texas (Amarillo) and the Western District of Texas (Wichita Falls)—guarantee assignment to a specific judge, enabling litigants to effectively select their decision-maker. Forum shopping has become a dominant feature of major constitutional and regulatory litigation, with both progressive and conservative litigants routing cases through jurisdictions with favorable judicial composition. [Observed]

**Mechanism in one sentence:** Federal venue rules allow strategic litigants to select courts and single-judge divisions that guarantee favorable judicial assignment for high-stakes constitutional challenges. [Observed]

### Actors and roles

- Plaintiffs' counsel select filing venues based on judicial composition, precedent favorability, procedural advantages, and the likelihood of obtaining preferred interim relief. [Observed]
- Single-judge division judges in targeted districts receive disproportionate shares of nationally significant cases due to venue-selection strategies. [Observed]
- The Judicial Conference of the United States issues policy recommendations on case assignment but lacks enforcement authority to mandate random assignment across divisions. [Observed]
- State attorneys general and advocacy organizations file strategic challenges in selected jurisdictions to maximize doctrinal impact. [Observed]
- Congress sets venue statutes and could modify jurisdictional rules to constrain forum shopping, but rarely acts due to political incentives on both sides. [Observed]

### Process map (bulleted)

- Litigants identify potential venues by analyzing judicial composition, local rules, case-assignment procedures, and appellate circuit preferences. [Observed]
- Cases are filed in jurisdictions offering the most favorable combination of trial judge ideology, circuit precedent, and procedural speed. [Observed]
- Single-judge divisions guarantee assignment to a known judge, eliminating the randomness that typically distributes cases among multiple judges. [Observed]
- Selected judges issue rulings—including nationwide injunctions—that have impact far beyond their geographic jurisdiction. [Observed]
- Appellate review occurs in the circuit where the case was filed, allowing litigants to select both trial and appellate forums through initial venue choice. [Observed]

### Where power concentrates

- **Gatekeepers:** Single-judge division judges become gatekeepers for entire categories of national litigation when strategic filing concentrates cases before them. [Observed]
- **Bottlenecks:** Federal venue statutes create bottlenecks by defining permissible filing locations broadly enough to enable forum selection among multiple jurisdictions. [Observed]
- **Veto points:** Congress could constrain forum shopping through venue-statute reform, but political beneficiaries of the practice on both sides create legislative gridlock. [Observed]

### Common failure modes

- Forum shopping undermines the legitimacy of federal adjudication by enabling litigants to predetermine outcomes through judge selection rather than merits-based argument. [Observed]
- Single-judge divisions concentrate nationally consequential decisions in judges who lack the resources, staff, and caseload support of multi-judge courts. [Observed]
- Competing forum-shopped injunctions from different courts create conflicting nationwide orders, producing legal uncertainty and forcing Supreme Court intervention. [Observed]
- The practice incentivizes escalation: as one side discovers favorable venues, opponents identify counter-venues, fragmenting national legal uniformity. [Observed]
