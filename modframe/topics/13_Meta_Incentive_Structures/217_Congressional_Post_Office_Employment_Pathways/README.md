# Congressional Post Office Employment Pathways

Purpose: Maps how congressional staff systematically transition to private sector roles that monetize their legislative knowledge and relationships, creating a one-way expertise pipeline from the public to the private sector.

Status: draft

Tags: revolving door, congressional staff, lobbying, compensation, brain drain, institutional capacity

Outline: see `outline.md`

## Key actors

- Congressional staff (current and former)
- Lobbying firms and corporate government affairs offices
- Members of Congress and committee chairs
- Trade associations
- Consulting firms specializing in government relations

## Statutes and authorities

- Honest Leadership and Open Government Act of 2007 (Pub. L. 110-81) (cooling-off periods)
- House and Senate ethics rules on post-employment restrictions
- Congressional office and committee budget authorities (staff salary constraints)

## Case studies

- Committee staff director departures from high-value committees (Finance, Appropriations, Energy and Commerce) to K Street firms
- Chiefs of staff salary trajectories: Hill ($170K) to lobbying ($350K+)
- Senior legislative aides who specialize in specific policy areas and depart for trade associations in the same field
