# Political Risk Insurance Markets

Purpose: Maps how political risk insurance (PRI) prices government behavior as an insurable risk, creating financial incentives that reward investor-friendly policies while using public agency guarantees to subsidize private international investment.

Status: draft

Tags: political risk, insurance, DFC, MIGA, investment, foreign policy, moral hazard

Outline: see `outline.md`

## Key actors

- U.S. International Development Finance Corporation (DFC, formerly OPIC)
- Multilateral Investment Guarantee Agency (MIGA / World Bank Group)
- Private PRI underwriters (Lloyd's syndicates, AIG, Zurich, Chubb)
- International investors (corporations, private equity, infrastructure funds)
- Host country governments
- Political risk consultants and rating agencies

## Statutes and authorities

- BUILD Act of 2018 (Pub. L. 115-254, Title VI) (DFC establishment)
- MIGA Convention (World Bank Group)
- DFC portfolio cap and investment guidelines

## Case studies

- DFC/OPIC expropriation claims and subrogation outcomes
- MIGA guarantees in politically unstable investment environments
- PRI coverage for infrastructure projects in high-risk jurisdictions
