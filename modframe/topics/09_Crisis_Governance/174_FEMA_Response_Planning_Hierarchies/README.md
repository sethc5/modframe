# FEMA Response Planning Hierarchies

Purpose: Maps how FEMA's response planning hierarchy creates standardized coordination across agencies and jurisdictions through NRF, NIMS, and ESFs, but its coordinating authority means response depends on voluntary cooperation rather than command.

Status: draft

Tags: FEMA, emergency management, NRF, NIMS, ESF, coordination, disaster response, interagency

Outline: see `outline.md`

## Key actors

- FEMA Administrator (coordination function)
- Federal Coordinating Officer (field operations)
- ESF primary agencies (15 Emergency Support Functions)
- State emergency management agencies
- Local emergency managers
- DHS Secretary (policy oversight)

## Statutes and authorities

- Post-Katrina Emergency Management Reform Act of 2006
- Robert T. Stafford Act (42 U.S.C. §§ 5121–5207)
- National Response Framework (4th ed., 2019)
- National Incident Management System (NIMS)

## Case studies

- Hurricane Katrina response failures and NRF development
- FEMA coordination during concurrent disasters (2017 hurricane season)
- ESF agency response gaps during major declarations
