# Continuity of Government Logistics

**Summary:** Continuity of Government logistics encompasses the physical infrastructure, communication systems, transportation plans, and personnel procedures required to relocate essential government operations to alternate facilities during catastrophic events — a largely classified operational layer that converts COG policy into executable capability. [Observed] The logistics architecture includes hardened facilities (Raven Rock Mountain Complex, Mount Weather Emergency Operations Center, Cheyenne Mountain, and others), redundant communication systems (including the Defense Information Systems Network and emergency satellite communications), and pre-positioned supplies and equipment. [Observed] The Federal Continuity Directive 1 (FCD 1) requires each federal agency to maintain a COOP plan that includes essential functions identification, orders of succession (at least three deep), delegations of authority, alternate facility identification, and reconstitution procedures. [Observed] The structural challenge is that COG logistics must be maintained in perpetual readiness for events that may never occur, creating a resource allocation tension between investing in catastrophic-event preparedness and addressing routine operational needs, while classification prevents public evaluation of whether the logistics system is actually adequate for its intended purpose. [Inferred]

**Mechanism in one sentence:** COG logistics maintain classified alternate facilities, communication systems, and personnel procedures in perpetual readiness for catastrophic events, but classification prevents evaluation of whether the system would actually work when needed. [Inferred]

### Actors and roles

- **FEMA** — manages the civilian COOP program, issues Federal Continuity Directives, and maintains Mount Weather Emergency Operations Center. [Observed]
- **Department of Defense** — maintains military COG facilities (Raven Rock, Cheyenne Mountain, NAOC airborne command posts); provides communication infrastructure and transportation assets. [Observed]
- **White House Military Office** — manages presidential COG logistics, including the Presidential Emergency Operations Center (PEOC), Air Force One, and the nuclear football. [Observed]
- **Agency COOP coordinators** — designated officials in each federal agency responsible for maintaining COOP plans, conducting exercises, and coordinating with FEMA. [Observed]
- **Secret Service** — manages protective logistics for the presidential succession chain during COG activation. [Observed]
- **Defense Information Systems Agency (DISA)** — provides communications infrastructure for COG operations, including secure voice, data, and video systems. [Observed]

### Process map (bulleted)

- Each federal agency maintains a COOP plan under FCD 1, identifying essential functions (Mission Essential Functions and Primary Mission Essential Functions), orders of succession, and alternate operating facilities. [Observed]
- FEMA conducts periodic assessments of agency COOP readiness and coordinates interagency continuity exercises (Eagle Horizon exercises). [Observed]
- COG facilities are maintained in operational readiness, including hardened structures, backup power, water, food, communication equipment, and classified document storage. [Observed]
- In a COG activation, designated personnel are transported to alternate facilities via pre-planned routes and transportation assets (helicopters, motorcades, and specialized aircraft). [Observed]
- Communication systems activate to connect alternate facilities with each other and with military command structures, ensuring presidential authority over nuclear forces and military operations. [Observed]
- Essential government functions continue at alternate facilities under delegated authority from relocated senior officials. [Observed]
- Reconstitution plans guide the return to normal operations once the threat is resolved; reconstitution timelines and procedures vary by agency and scenario. [Observed]

### Where power concentrates

- **Gatekeepers:** The White House Situation Room and FEMA's National Watch Center monitor for activation triggers; the decision to activate COG procedures rests with the President or designated successors. [Observed]
- **Bottlenecks:** Transportation capacity to alternate facilities is limited; in a no-notice event, the time between warning and impact may not allow full relocation. [Observed]
- **Veto points:** Classification prevents external evaluation of whether alternate facilities have adequate capacity, communication systems work as designed, or COOP plans are realistic. [Observed]

### Common failure modes

- COOP plans may be outdated, untested, or unrealistic because agencies treat them as compliance requirements rather than operational necessities. [Inferred]
- COG communication systems may fail under the electromagnetic and infrastructure disruption conditions they are designed to survive (limited real-world stress testing). [Inferred]
- The classification of COG logistics prevents public and most congressional evaluation of whether the system would function during an actual catastrophic event. [Observed]
- Personnel may not reach alternate facilities in time during no-notice events, particularly for agencies located far from their designated alternate facilities. [Inferred]
- Agency COOP plans focus on individual agency continuity but may not adequately address interagency coordination at alternate facilities. [Inferred]
- COG exercises (Eagle Horizon) test procedures but cannot fully simulate catastrophic conditions, leaving uncertainty about performance under actual stress. [Observed]

### What evidence would prove/disprove key claims

- Declassify COG exercise after-action reports to enable public evaluation of system readiness. [Observed]
- Audit agency COOP plan compliance with FCD 1 requirements and track remediation timelines for identified gaps. [Observed]
- Test communication system resilience under simulated electromagnetic pulse or infrastructure disruption conditions. [Inferred]
- Analyze transportation logistics for no-notice scenarios to determine realistic relocation timelines. [Inferred]
- Compare U.S. COG logistics with those of other NATO allies that have undergone more recent modernization. [Hypothesis]
