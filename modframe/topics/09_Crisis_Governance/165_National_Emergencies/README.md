# National Emergencies

Purpose: Maps how the National Emergencies Act creates a system where presidents accumulate emergency powers through declarations that Congress has never terminated, producing a permanent state of expanded executive authority.

Status: draft

Tags: emergency powers, NEA, executive authority, congressional oversight, national security, presidential power

Outline: see `outline.md`

## Key actors

- The President (declaration authority)
- Congress (termination mechanism, six-month review)
- Office of Legal Counsel (legal interpretation)
- Federal agencies (implementation)
- Courts (judicial review)
- Brennan Center for Justice (tracking and analysis)

## Statutes and authorities

- National Emergencies Act (50 U.S.C. §§ 1601–1651)
- International Emergency Economic Powers Act (50 U.S.C. §§ 1701–1707)
- 130+ statutory emergency powers across federal code
- Youngstown Sheet & Tube Co. v. Sawyer, 343 U.S. 579 (1952)

## Case studies

- Carter-era emergency declarations still active 40+ years later
- Trump border wall emergency declaration (2019) and congressional termination attempts
- Post-9/11 emergency declarations and indefinite renewal
