# War Powers Resolution Enforcement Limits

Purpose: Maps how the War Powers Resolution's 60-day withdrawal requirement has never been enforced by Congress or adjudicated by courts, making it a textually powerful but structurally inoperative constraint on presidential military authority.

Status: draft

Tags: war powers, military authority, AUMF, presidential power, Congress, enforcement, constitutional

Outline: see `outline.md`

## Key actors

- The President (military initiation)
- Congress (war declaration power, AUMFs)
- OLC (legal interpretation)
- Senate Foreign Relations / House Foreign Affairs Committees
- Federal courts (political question doctrine)

## Statutes and authorities

- War Powers Resolution (50 U.S.C. §§ 1541–1548)
- 2001 AUMF (Pub. L. 107-40)
- 2002 Iraq AUMF (Pub. L. 107-243)
- U.S. Constitution, Article I § 8 (war declaration) / Article II § 2 (Commander in Chief)

## Case studies

- Libya 2011 intervention without congressional authorization
- 2001 AUMF geographic and temporal expansion across 20+ years
- Yemen military involvement and War Powers debates
