# Stafford Act Disaster Authority

Purpose: Maps how the Stafford Act channels federal disaster aid through a discretionary presidential declaration process that is structurally susceptible to political influence despite being framed as technical assessment.

Status: draft

Tags: Stafford Act, disaster, FEMA, presidential discretion, emergency declaration, federal aid, intergovernmental

Outline: see `outline.md`

## Key actors

- The President (declaration authority)
- State governors (request initiation)
- FEMA (damage assessment, response coordination, aid administration)
- Congress (supplemental appropriations)
- Local governments (first response, damage reporting)

## Statutes and authorities

- Robert T. Stafford Disaster Relief and Emergency Assistance Act (42 U.S.C. §§ 5121–5207)
- Sandy Recovery Improvement Act of 2013
- Disaster Recovery Reform Act of 2018 (DRRA)

## Case studies

- Hurricane Katrina declaration and response failures (2005)
- Electoral competitiveness correlation with declarations (Reeves 2011)
- COVID-19 nationwide emergency and Stafford Act activation
