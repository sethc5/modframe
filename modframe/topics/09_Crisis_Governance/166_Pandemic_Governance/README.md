# Pandemic Governance

Purpose: Maps how pandemic governance fragments authority across overlapping federal agencies, statutory frameworks, and federal-state coordination structures, creating a response system whose effectiveness depends on political coordination rather than structural design.

Status: draft

Tags: pandemic, public health, emergency response, CDC, FDA, FEMA, federalism, coordination

Outline: see `outline.md`

## Key actors

- HHS Secretary (public health emergency declaration)
- CDC (surveillance, guidance, quarantine authority)
- FDA (emergency use authorization)
- FEMA (response logistics)
- State governors (primary public health authority)
- The President (NEA/Stafford Act declarations)
- Congress (emergency appropriations)

## Statutes and authorities

- Public Health Service Act (42 U.S.C. § 247d)
- Federal Food, Drug, and Cosmetic Act, § 564 (21 U.S.C. § 360bbb-3) (EUA)
- Stafford Act (42 U.S.C. §§ 5121–5207)
- National Response Framework, ESF #8
- 42 CFR Parts 70/71 (quarantine and isolation)
