# Pandemic Response Chains of Authority

Purpose: Maps how pandemic response authority fragments across parallel tracks (public health, countermeasure, logistics) and between federal and state actors, making effectiveness dependent on political coordination rather than command structure.

Status: draft

Tags: pandemic, chain of authority, coordination, CDC, FDA, FEMA, federal-state, political interference

Outline: see `outline.md`

## Key actors

- White House task force (ad hoc coordination)
- HHS Secretary (PHE authority)
- CDC Director (surveillance and guidance)
- FDA Commissioner (EUA decisions)
- ASPR / BARDA (countermeasure development)
- FEMA Administrator (logistics coordination)
- State and local health officials

## Statutes and authorities

- Public Health Service Act (42 U.S.C. § 247d)
- National Response Framework, ESF #8
- Federal Food, Drug, and Cosmetic Act (EUA provisions)
