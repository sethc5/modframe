# National Security Emergency Declarations

Purpose: Maps how IEEPA and national security emergency declarations function as permanent presidential foreign policy tools disguised as temporary emergency measures, with minimal congressional oversight or judicial review.

Status: draft

Tags: IEEPA, sanctions, national security, OFAC, emergency powers, foreign policy, presidential authority

Outline: see `outline.md`

## Key actors

- The President (emergency declaration)
- Treasury Department / OFAC (sanctions implementation)
- State Department (diplomatic coordination)
- National Security Council (policy coordination)
- Financial institutions (compliance enforcement)
- Congress (oversight, rarely exercised)

## Statutes and authorities

- International Emergency Economic Powers Act (50 U.S.C. §§ 1701–1707)
- National Emergencies Act (50 U.S.C. §§ 1601–1651)
- Specific statutory sanctions (Iran, North Korea, Russia)

## Case studies

- IEEPA sanctions programs renewed for decades
- OFAC SDN list growth and due process challenges
- Extraterritorial secondary sanctions and diplomatic friction
