# Continuity of Government Planning

Purpose: Maps how Continuity of Government planning creates a classified parallel governance architecture for catastrophic events, operating outside democratic accountability because the scenarios it anticipates may suspend constitutional structures.

Status: draft

Tags: continuity of government, COG, COOP, presidential succession, classified, nuclear, catastrophic events

Outline: see `outline.md`

## Key actors

- The President (COG policy through directives)
- National Security Council (coordination)
- FEMA (civilian COOP program management)
- DOD / NORTHCOM (military support, facilities)
- Congress (weakest continuity provisions)
- Designated survivors (succession continuity)

## Statutes and authorities

- NSPD-51/HSPD-20 (National Continuity Policy, 2007)
- Presidential Succession Act (3 U.S.C. § 19)
- 25th Amendment (presidential disability and succession)
- FEMA Federal Continuity Directives (FCD 1, FCD 2)

## Case studies

- COG activation on 9/11 (partial activation, classified details)
- Congressional continuity vulnerability analysis
- Presidential succession constitutional questions (Speaker/President pro tempore eligibility)
