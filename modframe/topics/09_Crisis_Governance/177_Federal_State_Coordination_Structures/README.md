# Federal State Coordination Structures

Purpose: Maps how federal-state coordination during emergencies operates through voluntary frameworks that constitutional federalism prevents from becoming unified command, making response quality dependent on political alignment and personal relationships.

Status: draft

Tags: federal-state, coordination, EMAC, federalism, JFO, National Guard, intergovernmental, emergency management

Outline: see `outline.md`

## Key actors

- FEMA (federal coordination interface)
- State governors (constitutional authority)
- State emergency management agencies
- EMAC (interstate mutual aid compact)
- FCO / SCO (paired coordination officers)
- National Guard (dual-status operations)
- FEMA Regional Administrators

## Statutes and authorities

- Stafford Act (42 U.S.C. §§ 5121–5207)
- Emergency Management Assistance Compact (EMAC)
- National Response Framework (4th ed., 2019)
- 32 U.S.C. / 10 U.S.C. (National Guard status)

## Case studies

- Multi-state disaster EMAC deployments (2017 hurricane season)
- Federal-state coordination failures by partisan alignment
- Dual-status commander operations during major disasters
